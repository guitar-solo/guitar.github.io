In late 2023, I purchased a 2010s [Godin xtSA multi-voice](https://godinguitars.com/product/xtsa-lightburst-flame/) electric guitar from a seller on 
Reverb located in Bethlehem, PA. A well-taken-care-of instrument with a stock setup
featuring: a solid core body style with silver leaf maple center and poplar wings, a flame 
maple leaftop, mahogany neck, [richlite](https://www.richlite.com/) fingerboard, 
floating tremolo bridge, [RMC](https://www.rmcpickup.com/products.html) piezo transducer 
saddles, Godin tuning machine heads with locking caps, a [Graph Tech TUSQ](https://graphtech.com/pages/tusq-nuts) 
nut, and a 13-pin output. It also came with a [Mooradian](https://mooradian.com/) padded guitar bag.

**Specifications:**

* Fingerboard Radius : 16" (406.4 mm)
* Scale Length : 25.5" (647.7 mm)
* Nut Width : 1.72" (43.688 mm)
* Frets : 22
* Machine Head Ratio : 18:1

![sketching the shape for a custom pickguard](/notebooks/sketching-pickguard-shape.jpg)

I've always been impressed with the body shape of Godin xtSA guitars. The body would serve as a
solid design base for customization. The first step in the customization process was sketching a
pickguard shape that emphasized the original Godin body curvature while pushing the science fiction
elements of the design to the edge.

**First** -- The first iteration set the overall strategy. The curves felt tight and compressed. Too linear,
angular, WWII German U-boat ideations. A solid beginning needing refinement and sophistication.

![pickguard first draft](/notebooks/pickguard-draft-1.jpg)

**Second** -- The second iteration highlighted the missing elements. Emphasizing a shapely, feminine figure. 
Curvy, energetic, and reminicent of childlike bubbles. And yet, the proportions felt off and 
overly exaggerated.

![pickguard second draft](/notebooks/pickguard-draft-2.jpg)

**Third** -- The third iteration was slightly more elongated, with smoother and less abrupt curves. 
This design simultaneously felt refined, sophisticated, and futuristic. This was the balance of 
perfection that I was after.

![pickguard third draft](/notebooks/pickguard-draft-3.jpg)

The third iteration determined to be the final design for the pickguard.
