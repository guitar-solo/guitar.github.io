Used the [Bosch ROS20-VSK](https://www.boschtools.com/us/en/products/ros20vsk-0601387511) variable 
speed, random orbital sander to polish the steel pickguard before surface mounting any electronics. A
multi-stage process of steadily increasing the grit of the 5-inch wet/dry silicon carbide sanding 
discs:

<div class="d-flex justify-content-center fw-200 mb-5">
80 -> 120 -> 180 -> 220 -> 400 -> 600 -> 800
</div>

![pickguard second draft](/notebooks/pickguard-mirrored-1.jpg)

![pickguard second draft](/notebooks/pickguard-mirrored-2.jpg)

While I originally wanted a mirrored finish to adorn the surface of my instrument, I veered. I used 
the 800 grit paper to etch a faux Mac Worthington design into the pickguard to exude a controlled, 
chaotic energy; rather than a detached pretentiousness.
