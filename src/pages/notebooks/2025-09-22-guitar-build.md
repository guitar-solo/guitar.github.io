Why aren't the volume potentiometers of the sliding varietal, rather than the common rotational 
mechanism? That would be due to the shifting costs to import electronic components into the 
United States. Significantly increased tariff rates have been imposed on electronic components,
impacting US consumers across the board. Small purchases, like a handful of components needed to
build a custom electronic guitar, have become increasingly cost prohibitive. The political and
administrative environment surrounding the imposition and collection tariffs at the border is
chaotic, adding to the confusing nightmare that is selecting the best options for components.

"We no longer ship to the United States" has been getting in the way of completing the build.
There's a lot a preparatory research involved in a guiar project. Results in a non-trivial 
delay between performing market research, considering design trade-offs for budget and 
availability, selecting the ideal set of functional components, and initiating the purchase
of necessary components. Unfortunately, I've had to make compromises. Not critical to overall
functional outcomes, but frustating after such a long process.

Since I wasn't able to obtain the sliding potentiometers, these standard 500k audio taper 
rotational-type are likely getting mounted internally, upside-down from the backplate, and 
set for a fixed balance of source signals.
